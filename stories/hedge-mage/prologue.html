The Cradle shimmered. Ribbons of emerald and violet stretched across the sky in a slow, rhythmic pulse. Ever watchful, it cast its light across the land, illuminating the fields of golden grain swaying in the night breeze. Shadows stretched long and thin from an ancient sycamore tree, its gnarled roots curling deep into the soil, leaching for something long forgotten.

A lone figure knelt beneath the tree, hands trembling as they pressed a small leather-bound book into a hole in the earth. The pages, thick with ink and time, bore the gentle glow of old magic. Magic meant to be read with small hands and eager eyes, magic meant to be traced into the dirt with a child's wonder. The weight of grief bowed the man's shoulders, but the movement was careful, deliberate, as if the act itself might stave off loss.

The child was gone, taken by a sickness that no spell could mend. There'd been no cries, no resistance — only a slow fading, a light dimming too soon. But magic was never truly lost. The land remembered. The sky carried whispers of what had come before. And beneath this ancient sycamore, where roots and earth intertwined, there would be memory.

The soil was pressed firm beneath calloused fingers, sealing the book away, a last offering to the world. Not to be lost, not to be forgotten. One day, another child would find it. Perhaps not soon, not in his lifetime, but the magic woven into the pages would call to them — one with the same hunger for knowledge, the same restless curiosity. And when they did, they would not be alone.

Something stirred beneath the roots. Not seen. Not heard. Felt. A presence as old as the land itself, watching, waiting. The Cradle's light pulsed once — slow, rhythmic — like a heartbeat in the sky. The air held its breath, and the moment passed.

The figure stood, dusting soil from their hands, casting one last glance at the tree. The night wind carried the scent of tilled soil and the rain to come. In the distance, the fields whispered. The land would remember.

And when the time came, the child who found the book would be drawn not just by chance, but by unseen hands of the past.

Not just to learn.

But to awaken.

The Cradle flickered, and the world turned onward…forgetting. Until it was time to remember again.
